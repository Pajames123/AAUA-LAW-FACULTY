Undergraduate Admissions

Admission into the Faculty of Law is highly competitive. Candidates are expected to possess high academic standards and moral character. We offer admission via UTME (100 Level) and Direct Entry (200 Level).

UTME Admission (100 Level)

O'Level Requirements (WAEC/NECO/GCE)

Candidates must possess at least five (5) Credit passes at not more than two sittings.

  • English Language (Compulsory)
  • Literature-in-English (Compulsory)
  • Mathematics (At least a Credit/Pass)
  • Any other 2 Arts or Social Science subjects (e.g., Govt, CRS/IRS, History).
UTME Subject Combination

In JAMB, you must write the following subjects:

1. English Language
2. Literature-in-English
3. Any two Arts/Social Science subjects (e.g., Government, CRS/IRS).

Direct Entry (200 Level)

In addition to the O'Level requirements listed above, Direct Entry candidates must satisfy one of the following:

  • First Degree: A minimum of Second Class (Upper Division) in a related discipline from a recognized University.
  • JUPEB / A-Levels: A minimum of 13 points in relevant Arts/Social Science subjects.
  • Note: The Faculty generally does not accept Diploma results for Direct Entry admission into Law.

How to Apply

1
JAMB Registration

Register for UTME and choose Adekunle Ajasin University, Akungba-Akoko (AAUA) as your first choice.

2
Post-UTME Screening

Upon meeting the general University cut-off mark, purchase the Post-UTME form via putme.aaua.edu.ng.

3
Admission Status

Check your CAPS on the JAMB portal and the AAUA admission portal regularly for updates.
